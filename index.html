Busy and productive are not the same thing. I learned that working in human services before I ever opened a business. We had teams logging 12-hour days and still falling behind. Not because people weren't working hard. Because the systems they were working inside were designed to create activity, not outcomes. The same pattern shows up in every consulting engagement I take on. Founders who can't take a vacation. Executives answering Slack at 11pm. Teams in motion but not in progress. Here's the shift that changes everything: Stop measuring effort. Start measuring what moved. In human services we called it case progress. In business I call it operational velocity. The question is always the same — did what you did today move the dial, or did it just keep the machine running? If you're spending more than 70% of your week maintaining instead of building, your business is managing you. Not the other way around. That's the problem OIA solves.
